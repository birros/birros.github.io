Why choose hugo for my static website

On 12/28/2017 by Birros

To inaugurate the journal I would like to explain the technical and technological choices used to publish this website.

No question of cluttering myself with a dynamic website (opens new window), meaning maintenance and insecurity, or a database (opens new window) and worst yet a heavy framework (opens new window). My first choice was Jekyll (opens new window), fulfilling the first two criteria, until I discovered Hugo (opens new window).

In addition to checking these same criteria, Hugo is a simple executable (opens new window) allowing a complete and fast development, for my needs, without requiring the installation of a plug-in (opens new window) to have essential functionalities such as multilingualism, RSS feeds (opens new window), or the ability to organize my content in sections.

Apart from rewriting (opens new window) a few templates (opens new window) of the Ananke (opens new window) theme to allow navigation between English and French, the implementation consists of providing some informations in the configuration file, such as the name of the website and the languages used, and arranging its content, written in Markdown (opens new window), in the content folder.

The Ananke theme is the one given as example during the official documentation tutorial. Although a little too large for my taste, this one offers good looking effortlessly.

# Complements

No need to say that the use of free software (opens new window) was registered from the beginning. Are there still some popular proprietary software (opens new window) in the world of blogging (opens new window) ?

This solution largely satisfies my needs of the moment, in the future the implementation of a comment system will be done, my choice going to Staticman (opens new window).

Hosting is provided by GitHub Pages (opens new window), the most judicious choice since the sources are hosted on GitHub (opens new window) and do not want to bother me with the maintenance of a physical (opens new window) or virtual machine (opens new window).

It is also the occasion to add a note, in the section of the website dedicated to this purpose, concerning Hugo's server mode and the local network.
